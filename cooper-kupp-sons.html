Cooper Kupp | Wife Anna Marie Kupp & Relationship

Kupp’s wife Anna (whose birthday is on March 22) is always by his side- from struggling days- to injuries- to celebration in the Super Bowl LVI victory with the LA Rams.

Family History As NFL Players

Cypress and Cooper Jameson’s father, Cooper Kupp, is a third-generation NFL player from the Kupp family- one of five families with three generations in the NFL.

Kupp’s father Craig Kupp (b. April 14, 1967), is a former football quarterback.

After Selah High School, he had a collegiate football career at Pacific Lutheran University and also became a 1986 All-Frontier Conference.

Craig then became the 5th round (135th overall) pick of the New York Giants in the 1990 NFL Draft but saw a waiver ahead of the season.

Then, he played for Phoenix Cardinals (1990-91) and Dallas Cowboys (1991-92) before his NFL departure.

Jake Kupp, Cooper Kupp’s grandfather (great-grandfather of Cypress & Cooper Jr.), is a former football guard and the 1991 New Orleans Saints Hall of Fame inductee.

An alum of the University of Washington, Jake joined Dallas Cowboys (from 1964 to 1965) as the 1964 NFL Draft pick.

After that, he played for Washington Redskins (1966) before joining Atlanta Falcons (1967) following a brief stint at New Orleans Saints.

In 1968, he would rejoin the Saints and play until retirement in 1975.

Brief On Cooper Kupp | NFL Draft, Contract & Salary

Born on June 15, 1993, Cooper Kupp began playing football when he was 9 years old, and his father described him as being very persistent and diligent.

Cooper won numerous contests and set numerous school records but graduated without receiving a football scholarship offer.

Later, Kupp enrolled at Eastern Washington University and finished four seasons with 6,464 receiving yards, 428 receptions & 71 touchdowns.

He saw accolades like Walter Payton Award and Jerry Rice Award.

The college proved home to NFL players like Kendrick Bourne, Samson Ebukam, Taiwan Jones, and Nsimba Webster.

Then, the Yakima-born became a third-round pick in the 2017 NFL Draft by Los Angeles Rams.

He signed a rookie contract with the Rams on June 13, 2017, worth $3.83 million for 4 years.

On September 12, 2020, Cooper inked a 3-year $47.25 million contract extension with the Rams.

And after the Rams won the Super Bowl LVI (of the 2021-22 season), he got another extension for 3 years worth $80.1 million on June 22, 2022.

The new contract spiked his annual salary to $10 million for the 2022-23 season, with a $20 million signing bonus.

Among the honorable accolades, the notable ones are the Super Bowl MVP 2021 and Pro Bowl 2021
