10 Winter Outfits for 2023, If Your Aesthetic Is Quiet Luxury

Quiet luxury may just be the loudest trend of the year. It girls began kissing loud logos goodbye as they opted for more subtle pieces—while making sure to inform everyone of the photos of Sofia Richie and Shiv from Succession on their mood boards. We witnessed a swarm of crisp button-down shirts tucked into trousers, white linen dresses paired with Mary Janes, and neutral-toned outfits styled with understated accessories.

Now that wintry temperatures have come to greet us once again, it’s time to trade in our collection of lightweight garments and shorter hemlines for pieces that can withstand the cold. If you’re wondering how to do so while still maintaining the quiet luxury aesthetic, it’s actually quite simple.

“Quiet luxury is all about quality craftsmanship, elevated fabrics, and a celebration of timeless style,” says Bloomingdale’s fashion director Marissa Galante Frank. “Minimalism is a key pillar of quiet luxury—less embellishment allows the fabrics and styling to be the focus of the look.” Quiet luxury, she adds, celebrates capsule wardrobes and investment pieces that you can wear for years to come.

So what does quiet luxury look like during the winter? Galante Frank says “a chic tall boot, a cashmere sweater, and a beautiful leather tote” are just the start. Ahead, you’ll find all of that and more.

The Classic Trench

Kirstin Sinclair

When you think of timeless outerwear, trench coats should instantly come to mind. The classic silhouette of this style has survived centuries of cyclical trends, and every fashion connoisseur will agree that a quality trench has the power to elevate any outfit. With a turtleneck, trousers, and sleek black boots, you simply can’t go wrong.
