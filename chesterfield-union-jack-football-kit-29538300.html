'Embarrassing' Union Jack football kit is slammed as fans slate inspiration from 1800s

Chesterfield have launched a daring new Union Jack football kit design - and fans were left disgusted at the “embarrassing” call-back to the 1800s.

The National League side took to Twitter on Friday to announce the drop of their new strip. The polo shirt in question was unlike any other, sporting the colours of the Union Jack flag with a Chesterfield badge across the centre.

It marked a nod to the jerseys the side wore 130 years ago, with adult and kids tops going for £45 and £35 a pop respectively. Yet the majority of the Spireites faithful in the comments section believed the kit should’ve been left firmly in the past, before slamming the design.

The official Chesterfield account had tweeted: “Our new Union Jack polo shirts will be in stock soon!

“Trivia fans - Chesterfield Town wore a Union Jack away shirt in 1892/93. Please let us know your thoughts, Spireites."

One Twitter user did not hold back in his scathing criticism, replying: “I’ll be honest with my thoughts. Cancel the order. They’re horrific and you’ll sell under 5.”

Another fan tweeted: “Embarrassing … a lot has changed since 1892/93, and like those two years, this is a shirt that should be consigned to the history books IMHO.”

Do you think the Union Jack design on a jersey is a bad move? Let us know in the comments section.

A third commented: “That is horrendous and a marketing mistake, a big one,” while a fourth added: “When you have the football at 3 but want your country back at 6.”

The original 19th century jerseys came with a stunning story, having being found in the loft of the Spital Hotel and donated to the club. They are believed to have belonged to the defunct Spital Olympic club.

Chesterfield first donned their Union Jack kit on November 1889 until the end of the 1893/94 season.
