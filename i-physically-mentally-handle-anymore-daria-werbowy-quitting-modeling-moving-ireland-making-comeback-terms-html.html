Daria Werbowy on quitting modeling, moving to Ireland, and making a comeback

'I physically and mentally couldn't handle it anymore': Daria Werbowy on quitting modeling, moving to Ireland, and making a comeback on her own terms

Supermodel Daria Werbowy has admitted that she 'physically and mentally couldn't handle' modeling when she quit the industry two years ago.

The 30-year-old, who covers the new issue of Harper's Bazaar, had been modeling since she was 14, and says she 'took a risk' when she decided to take a hiatus.

She told the magazine: 'I took a risk a couple of years ago where I decided to do the minimal amount that I could get away with, because I physically and mentally couldn’t handle it anymore.

Back on the cover: Daria Werbowy has made a return to modeling following a two-year break

Explaining that decision, she continued: 'It's like, you have everything you want, what you dreamt of is coming true, you can’t buy anything else to make you happy, so then you think, Well, what else is there? And you have to leave.'

The Canadian beauty, whose family is of Ukrainian descent, took time out to travel the world, eventually settling in Cork, Ireland, where she currently lives with her boyfriend, a carpenter.

'Never in a million years did I think I’d be living there, but I never thought I'd be a successful model either. I never thought I'd fall in love with who I’m in love with.'

She is cagey about the man in question, simply joking: 'It's weird—he's a female priest. Me and Sinead, we're supertight.'

New life: The Canadian beauty, whose family is of Ukrainian descent, took time out to travel the world, eventually settling in Cork, Ireland

Now, she says, she has a better sense of work-life balance.

'I work for a chunk of time, I do everything back-to-back, and then I’m completely off,' she explained.

The model says she stays in shape with yoga.

'Ashtanga yoga,' she specified. 'I'm addicted. I stopped drinking, so this is the way life goes.'

She is also a keen sailor, and even sailed across the Atlantic in 2008.

'I’m a pretty extreme person,' she said. 'if I say I’m going to sail across the Atlantic, I will.'
