Kelsey Grammers Daughter Mason Grammer Looks All Grown up on NYFW Catwalk

Is it just us, or did Mason Grammer literally grow up overnight? Kelsey Grammer‘s daughter hit the runway for NYFW on Thursday, Sept. 7 — and looked so stunning that we hardly recognized her all done up on the catwalk!

During a new interview, the 15-year-old girl opened up about her experience walking in Malan Breton’s 2017 show. “It always feels like the first time, it’s just an amazing experience,” Mason told Page Six.

Mason at NYFW.

Mason walked in both Breton’s February and September 2016 NYFW presentations as well as February 2017. Though she’s now an experienced model, the teenager admitted she still has one funny catwalk phobia. “I always get scared — I know this is weird — I get scared I’m gonna sneeze or something during it,” she previously said. “I don’t know why. When I’m walking I’m like, ‘Please do not sneeze.’ That’s my biggest fear.”

Kelsey, 61, welcomed Mason with his third wife, Camille Grammer, in October 2001. The Frasier star and Camille, 49 — a former Playboy model! — were previously married from 1997 until 2011.

According to Mason, her parents have been totally in favor of her following in their famous footsteps — so cool! “My dad was actually supportive [of me modeling], which is kind of surprising. But he’s happy for me, and I’ve told him every time I’ve gone [to NYFW] and he’s been really happy for me,” she said. “So that’s good. My mom is supportive about it [too].”

Mason and Kelsey in June 2016.

Camille — who also has a 13-year-old son, Jude, with Kelsey — loves watching her daughter fulfill her modeling dreams. “I am so proud, she’s a good person, she’s a good girl, in school she’s getting all A’s right now,” Camille said. “She’s been acing every test and we want to reward her with something that she loves and I think it helps her with her self-confidence.”

As far as Mason’s expectations for her modeling career, she says she’s just playing it by ear. “Just seeing where the road takes us,” she said, adding, “With school, I’ll try to stay on top of it and do the best that I can, ’cause I feel school comes first before any of this. It’s more fun right now and we’ll [see] if anything comes of it, but school is my number one priority.”
