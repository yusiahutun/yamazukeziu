Gov't Announces 2000 Job Vacancies for House Maids in Saudi Arabia with KSh 32k Salary, How to Apply

  • Kenyans without academic qualifications have been invited to take up domestic help positions in Saudi Arabia
  • The positions are 2,000 in number, with qualifications entailing the ability to speak in English and self-expression
  • President William Ruto has been advocating for the export of Kenyan labour to help strengthen the local currency through remittances

The Kenyan Ministry of Labour and Social Protection has announced several job vacancies for Kenyans with zero education and experience.

What are job opportunities in the Gulf?

Two thousand housemaid vacancies have been posted on the National Employment Authority Integrated Management (NEAIMS) with the requirements and application criteria.

The vacancies strictly for females are on a contract basis.

  • Salary amount

850 SAR (equivalent to KSh 33,000)

  • Contract period

Two years

  • Duties and responsibilities

General house cleaning

washing dishes

  • Knowledge/technical skills/expertise

- Ability to speak in English

- Ability to self-expression

- Hard worker

Interested candidates have until March 20 this year to submit their applications here.

Applicants with valid passports and good conduct certificates would have an added advantage.

President William Ruto has been advocating for massive exports of Kenyan labour, which he argues would revive the economy through remittances.

Why is Ruto seeking jobs for Kenyans abroad?

The president is on record underscoring the importance of his international trips in saving the country's currency, which has been experiencing a gradual devaluation against the greenback.

According to Ruto, his numerous overseas travels, primarily aimed at finding job opportunities, would align well with the government's plans to reinstate exchange rates through the inflow of diaspora remittances.

"Right now, there are those going to Israel this month, and another team will travel in the next month. Those going abroad will bring us dollars to deal with the exchange rates. Is there a problem with working in Israel for a salary between KSh 100,000 and 150,000?" posed the president at a previous church function.

He added:

"We want this country to move forward through hard work and a lot of Kenyans in employment. We want to increase our diaspora remittances from KSh 4 billion to KSh 10 billion every year."
