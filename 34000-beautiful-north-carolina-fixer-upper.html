Got $34,000? This Beautiful North Carolina Fixer-Upper Has Your Name on It

Here's one for all you lovers of old homes and small town life (and those who aren't aren't afraid of a little DIY). For just a little more than the cost of an SUV, you could be the owner of not one, but two beautiful fixer-uppers in North Carolina. They've got history, they've got bones, and they're in need of a little love.

Nancy Winslow

Hamilton, NC is a small, riverfront town in in the eastern part of the state, bordering the Roanoke River National Wildlife Refuge. Once a thriving commercial port, the town's history lives on in the beautiful antebellum homes that make up its National Register Historic District.

"It's a pretty town with many of the historic properties being restored and preserved," says Nancy Winslow of Keller Williams Realty, who is representing the property. The Roanoke River runs right along the edge of town and offers perfect recreation for hunters, fisherman and boaters."

Known as the Edmondson-Clark House, the main house for sale at 102 S Front Street was built in 1882 by Ebenezer Edmondson and was later owned by Dr. H. I. Clark, son of North Carolina's governor Henry T. Clark. At just over 1,400 sq. ft., it's a modest but charming house featuring original heart pine floors, elegant interior doors and wood trim and three antique fireplaces. The guest house in back was originally a commercial structure and was moved to the site around 1947. It is thought to have a served a number of a purposes in its lifetime, as home to a school for girls and, purportedly, the Bank of Hamilton. It's just dripping with potential!

There's some icing on the cake, too: The property's status on the National Register of Historic Places gives future owners the opportunity to apply for tax credits for renovation work.

"When you walk up on the wide front porch and go in the front door to a very large foyer, you can tell that this house has many stories to tell," says Winslow. "Whether it's morning coffee, afternoon tea or evening cocktails, this house is calling friends and family to visit here."
