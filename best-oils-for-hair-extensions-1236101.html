Hair extensions do not get oils from the scalp. Keep them healthy and shiny with the best oil for hair extensions.

Hair extensions are a fabulous way to give a new dimension to your natural hair. They are great to carry different hairstyles, adding volume and length, quickly preparing for a special occasion, correcting accidental haircut errors or masking hair loss. The follow-up care of hair extensions determines the lifespan and healthiness of the extensions. Washing, drying, detangling, styling, and oiling the extensions have to be done carefully and in the correct manner to maintain their longevity. 

Today, we will talk about oiling your hair extensions. It should always be done after styling the extensions to prevent the oil from heating up. But care should be taken while selecting the oils.

According to our contributor William Slator, Product developer at HairGuard - a holistic hair care product brand, “Frizziness in hair extensions can occur due to a lack of moisture or damage from heat styling. Proper care and maintenance, including regular conditioning and avoiding excessive heat, can help prevent frizz. When oiling hair extensions, it's essential to use a lightweight, nourishing oil and apply it sparingly, focusing on the mid-lengths to ends to avoid weighing down the hair or causing buildup.”

Using just any regular hair oil will not combat common hair extension problems like frizzy hair, breakage, dryness, entanglement, or lack of shine.     How to Choose the Right Oil for Hair Extensions?

    Choose the best oil for hair extensions by considering the following points:

    • Hair Type: The most effective oil for hair extensions will depend upon the types of hair. For fine and straight hair, light oils like argan or jojoba will best suit the extensions while for thick and curly hair, heavy oils like coconut or castor will suit the best.
    • Extension Type: The oil will also depend upon the type of hair extension you have. Temporary hair extensions like clip-in extensions do not require much oiling but permanent extensions need special care and caution. 
    • Purpose: The intent of using the oil also plays a role in selecting the best oil. For glossy and shiny hair, argan oil is best suited for hair extensions while castor oil can be used to promote hair health by improving the hair texture and condition the hair cuticles.
    • Ingredients: All-natural ingredients are ideal for hair extensions. Oils that are pure and organic will provide softness to hair extensions, keeping them as well as your real hair healthy. Avoid using products that have harsh chemicals which can lead to build-ups or hair damage.
    • Compatibility: Another important factor to take into consideration is the compatibility of the oil with your hair extensions. Some oils can only be used on 100% human hair extensions. Some oils might cause discoloration or have damaging effect on the hair extensions. It is always advisable to consult your hairstylist before buying oil for extensions.
    • Customer Reviews: Go through the customer reviews to find the ideal oil for your hair type and hair extensions.

    The Benefit of Oil for Hair Extensions

    Hair extensions can get dry and brittle due to a lack of natural oils. Like your real hair, the extensions do not get the goodness of oil from the scalp. With the best oil for hair extensions, you get several benefits of oiling your extensions such as:

    • Improves texture: Oiling the extensions keep them looking luscious, soft, and shiny throughout their lifespan. It conditions the hair strands and improve their texture.
    • Increases longevity: Providing a nourishing layer of hair oil revives and refreshes the extensions. It gives them healthy nutrients increasing their longevity.

    • Reduces tangles: Oiling the extensions before going to bed ensures there are lesser tangles to deal with in the morning. Ad lesser tangles mean lesser pulling and tugging at the bonds, keeping the hair extensions safe.

    FAQ's

    Should you use hair oil for extensions?

    Yes, using hair oil for hair extensions is very much beneficial for them. It makes them healthy and long-lasting.

    Can I oil my hair if I have extensions?

    Yes, you can oil your hair even if you have extensions but extra care should be taken to oil them in the proper manner to keep them from slippage or damage.

    What oils should not be used for hair extensions?

    Coconut oil should not be used to oil hair extensions as it can break the attachments. Even if it is used, do not let it sit for too long.

    How can I keep my hair healthy while wearing extensions?

    Brushing and detangling each morning and before going to bed, washing twice a week, and oiling keep the hair healthy while wearing extensions. While washing, care should be taken to rinse off well and while brushing, avoid extreme pulling and tugging to keep the attachments safe.

    Is argan oil OK for hair extensions?

    Argan oil provides moisture to hair extensions, keeping them from becoming frizzy and dry.

    What ingredient is bad for extensions?

    Harsh chemicals like sulfate, paraben, and alcohol strip the extensions of their natural oils. Products containing these chemicals should not be used on the extensions.
