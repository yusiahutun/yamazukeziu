In Kenya, numerous vocalists have significantly impacted the music industry with the raw power of their vocals. And according to African Vibes, the following are the best singers in Kenya.

1. Bien of Sauti Sol

Bien Aime Baraza is one of the best male vocalists in Kenya, hands down. He is part of the successful Sauti Sol music group that has won different awards, including East Africa’s Most Stylish Male Artiste in 2015 and Most Stylish Male Artiste – Africa, among many more.

Bien is a vocalist, songwriter, guitarist, and pianist. The talented vocalist started his professional career in 2005 when they officially launched Sauti Sol. Their first song was titled Mafunzo ya Dunia.

Although he is still part of the Sauri Sol music group, at some point, the band members took some time off to focus on their solo careers but recently reunited and released even more songs.

While going solo, fans got a fresh taste of his vocals after he released great songs, including Bald Man Anthem, Mbwe Mbwe, Inauma, and much more. Bien is the most loved among Sauti Sol members because of his vocals and fantastic personality.

2. Suzanna Owiyo

Suzanna Owiyo is a veteran in the music industry and has proven to be one of the best. Her grandfather, a renowned Nyatiti player, introduced her to music at a young age. And indeed, she perfected the trade to become the best vocalist in the lakeside city of Kisumu.

She got her career breakthrough most unexpectedly when she requested to prepare a theme song for the opening ceremony of the centennial celebrations of the city of Kisumu. Suzanna Owiyo did a great job. She later worked with other industry moguls, including Ted Josiah, who helped her create an award-winning album.

3. Eric Wainaina

Eric Wainaina requires no introduction. He has cemented his name in the Kenyan music industry and is accepted and appreciated by people from all walks of life, irrespective of music preference.

Eric launched his career with his debut album Sawa Sawa, in 2001. His music is mostly a blend of the Benga rhythm and East African guitars, with some modern harmony.

Many people remember Eric Wainaina’s iconic song, Nchi ya Kitu Kidogo (Country of small things), which means bribe in the local slang. It became a national anthem. Other than this song, Eric has participated in numerous other projects and is one of the best vocalists of all time in Kenya.

4. Nviri The Storyteller

Nviri the Storyteller has proven to be one of the best Kenyan vocalists, with his songs consistently topping charts. On top of being a vocalist, Nviri is a songwriter, guitarist, and video editor. The musician signed to Sauti Sol’s record label, Sol Generation Records.

Nviri gained prominence in the Kenyan music industry in 2018 after co-writing the famous song Melanin for Sauti Sol. The song appears in the band’s 2019 full-length album Afrikan Sauce.

The vocalist's song, Pombe Sigara, released in 2019, was his breakthrough in the music industry, and he followed it up with other popular songs, including Fore, Kessi Baadae and Lesotho. One thing about Nviri is that he has a distinct voice that distinguishes him from others in the music industry.

5. Nadia Mukami

Nadia Mukami ranks as one of the best female vocalists in Kenya. She is renowned for her hit singles, Radio Love, featuring Arrow Bwoy and Si Rahisi. Nadia Mukami released her first EP, African Popstar, in October 2020, which was well-received by her fans.

Nadia Mukami is a Maseno University graduate and finance expert by training. She is also a philanthropist, businesswoman, and a mother. She owns Sevens Creative Hub, a Kenyan record label company.

6. Nikita Kering

Nikita's vocals are so great that you can listen to her repeatedly. Her melodious voice earned her the prestigious Best Female Artist East Africa award for the 2021 All Africa Music Awards. And that is not all; she has won Best New Artist in the 2018 Pulse Music Video Awards and much more.

Nikita Kering started her music career at an early age and has nurtured her voice over the years to become one of the best vocalists in the country.

7. Jovial

Did you know that Jovial started his music journey as a choir member in Kisauni, Mombasa? Today, the female vocalist has numerous hits topping charts in Kenya and East Africa. Jovial learnt from the best, as her father was a guitarist and mother a choirmaster, serving at the Anglican Church of Kenya.

Jovial rose to fame when she released Chanda Chema under SQ Records in 2015. Later in 2019, she met Otile Brown and was signed to his record label, Just In Love Music Label. She was the first artist signed under the record label and released two singles with Otile Brown.

8. H_Art, the Band

H_Art, the Band, is a collection of talented Kenyan vocalists that have taken the music industry by storm. The lead singer, Mordecai Mwinyi’s melodious voice makes him one of the best male singers in the country. Other band members include Wachira Gatama, the poet, and Kenneth Muya, the guitarist.

9. Bensoul

Bensoulis another amazing male vocalist affiliated with Sauti Sol’s record label Sol Generation Records. He is multitalented as he plays various musical instruments and even writes songs. While growing up in Embu town, Bensoul got into music at a young age.

10. Otile Brown

Otile Brown is a music genius. He sings, writes and even has his record label. The way he delivers his vocals, you might think he spends most of his time in the studio, which would not be a surprise considering how much he loves his craft.
