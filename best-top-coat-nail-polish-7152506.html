The 12 Best Top Coat Nail Polish of 2024

Have you ever spent ages painting your own nails, only for them to lose their luster and chip within a day? Or, worse, have you paid to get your nails done somewhere and faced the same less-than-stellar results? Hey, we’ve all been there and there’s no doubt about it — it’s frustrating. Thankfully, it’s an easily-avoidable situation. The trick? Using the right nail polish top coat.

“Top coat finishes the manicure and helps make it last longer, giving it that finished look, and also adds a protective layer to the nail,” says celebrity manicurist Julie Kandalec, whose clients include Selena Gomez, Madelyn Cline, and Anya Taylor Joy. While many polishes today claim to be 3-in-1 formulas with base and top coats built in, Kandalec says a designated top coat is key to a long-lasting, beautiful-looking mani. “Some top coats offer a thicker barrier to give you that gel manicure look, some offer non-yellowing elements, and others are strengthening,” she reveals. “You can't have all of those properties mixed in with the color as well, because too many ingredients in one will dilute the product.” 

Suffice to say, adding a high-quality top coat to your nail polish collection is a must. If you’re looking for specific qualities, like chip-resistance, strengthening properties, or quick-drying abilities, we’ve got you covered. Ahead, find the 12 best top coats for nail polish, according to professional nail artists.

What to Keep in Mind

Finish

The finish of the top coat you choose will determine the overall finish of your manicure. So, it should go without saying, but if you want a glossy manicure, don’t opt for a matte finish and vice versa. Matte top coats are harder to find, but OPI Matte Top Coat is a favorite amongst nail professionals and novices alike.

Certifications

Top coats can carry a number of certifications, including vegan, cruelty-free, five-, seven-, eight-, 15-, and 21-free. Where vegan formulas are made without any animal byproducts and cruelty-free formulas are made without harming any animals, five-, seven-, eight-, 15-, and 21-free formulas refer to being made without common nail polish toxins, like ibutyl phthalate (DBP), toluene, formaldehyde, formaldehyde resin, camphor, ethyl tosylamide, and/or xylene. 

Ultimately, these ‘free’ formulas are better for your nails. Olive & June Super Glossy Top Coat has the highest certification on our list with 15-free.

Your Questions, Answered

Do I need a top coat for nail polish?

TL;DR: Yes. 

For starters, Kandalec says that top coats speed up overall manicure drying time. Beyond that, Minerva says that, “Top coat is essential to a long-lasting manicure. Not only does it help it last longer by encapsulating the color, but it provides a glossy outer coating that’s critical to finishing a nail look. Your manicure will lose its longevity if you do not add top coat.” 

What does top coat do for your nails?

In addition to making your nails look extra shiny, Minerva says that top coats are protective. “It creates a barrier between the nail color and your daily activities, helping to prevent chipping,” she explains.

Is top coat healthy for nails?

Generally speaking, yes. For the healthiest top coats for your nails, look for formulas free of common toxins, like ibutyl phthalate (DBP), toluene, formaldehyde, formaldehyde resin, camphor, ethyl tosylamide, and/or xylene. A simple way to do so is to look for five-, seven-, and eight-free certifications (though, some polishes nix even more questionable ingredients, like Olive & June Super Glossy Top Coat, which is 15-free).

Is top coat the same thing as a clear coat for nail polish?

No. Clear nail polish could just as easily be a base coat or strengthening treatment. “Most base coats are clear as well but tend to have a bit more of a rubberized finish for the color to lay on the nails more durably; they also have less sheen,” Minerva says. “Top coats are typically thicker and also have a high gloss finish.”

That said, it’s important to look for formulas specifically marketed as top coats. “Clear coat can be worn alone and is meant to compliment the natural nail; it’s not formulated to protect against chips and peeling,” Lee says. “Top coat, on the other hand, is formulated to protect the nail color from chips, peeling, and fading so you can achieve a long-lasting mani.”
