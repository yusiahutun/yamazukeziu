Dua Lipa 'will embark on her first stadium tour next year' after huge success of her Future Nostalgi

She is having an incredible year after being nominated for two Grammy Awards including for Song of the Year for Dance The Night. 

And now Dua Lipa is eyeing up to take on her first stadium tour next year as she prepares to head out on the road for the second time. 

The singer, 28, is looking to make the leap from arena shows following her successful 2022 Future Nostalgia tour, which saw her perform 12 shows in the UK.

Her team are reportedly trying to 'pencil in' the dates for next summer but as of yet nothing has been confirmed.  

A source told The Sun: 'Dua is going to be everywhere in 2024 - currently her team are looking at pencilling in her first ever stadium shows next summer.

'Her 2022 Future Nostalgia tour had 12 arena shows in the UK so a couple of massive stadium gigs is definitely possible. Her star has only grown since then. 

'Right now no dates are confirmed and locked in but it's on the cards.' 

Her second studio album Future Nostalgia was released in March 2020 and earned her three Grammy Awards, including the coveted Album Of The Year gong, at the 2021 ceremony.  

It comes after the pop star was The Mail on Sunday exclusively revealed that Dua, Madonna and Coldplay are the leading contenders to headline Glastonbury in 2024. 

After months of discussion, the three iconic singers are reportedly set to appear on the Pyramid Stage, though the official line-up is yet to be revealed.

One festival insider said: 'It's the dream line-up, a brilliant mix of different music and different atmospheres. Then, of course, you have Madonna on the final night, which will be epic.'

The usual eagle-eyed detective work from fans has also seen Ed Sheeran emerge as a possible headliner at Worthy Farm. 

When it comes to releasing the lineup, Glastonbury's organisers are notoriously good at keeping an expectant crowd in the dark over who might appear at Worthy Farm.

The exciting news comes after Dua recently revealed her upcoming 'personal' album is about dating and finding out whether a lover is 'real or not'.

Dua has been dating her current boyfriend Romain Gavras, 42, since the start of the year and was ­previously in a relationship with Anwar Hadid.

She explained on US radio: 'It's more the ­different feelings and ­emotions you have throughout the period of being single.

'It's also understanding. You understand what you want and what you need and what you deserve.

'It goes from the phase of ­figuring out ­singledom into potentially a new relationship.

'Then it's early days and ­getting to see the truth behind someone – whether they are real or not. Or who they are.'

Dua rose to fame with her debut album, Dua Lipa, and her latest album, Future Nostalgia, which just surpassed 10 billion streams.

She is reportedly putting the finishing touches to her third album.

The pop star has spent hundreds of hours in the recording studio in between sections of her world tour.

Dua hopes to release new music before the end of 2023 and her record company bosses are desperately trying to ensure recordings for the highly-anticipated LP are secure after a song she previously worked on with Pharrell Williams was leaked.
