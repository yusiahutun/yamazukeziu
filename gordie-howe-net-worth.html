Gordie Howe | Career

Howe debuted in the NHL on October 16, 1946, wearing the number 17 jersey. For the Detroit Red Wings, he started as a right-winger. But, when Roy Conacher left the team, he picked his number 9.

During the first three years, he had a total of 35 goals. Howe was a consistent scorer from then on. Starting in 1949, Howe was one of the leading scorers in the NHL, which he held for the next two decades.

However, Howe’s career almost came to an end in 1950. Howe collided with Ted Kennedy and flew headfirst into the sideboards

This accident happened in the first playoff game against the Toronto Maple Leafs. He had a concussion due to a fractured skull, and his cheekbone and nose were also broken.

Howe returned the next season. The concern was whether he would still be as ferocious and aggressive as before.

Accordingly, Howe led the league in goals, assists, and total points that season. And, he started every game in the NHL that season.

Eventually, he retired from the Red Wings in 1971 at the age of forty-three. In 1973, his sons Marty and Mark were signed by the Houston Aeros from the World Hockey Association. It was truly a dream come true for him to play on the same professional team as his boys.

Therefore, he got in shape and led his team to the WHA championship. Additionally, he scored 100 points, won the league’s Most Valuable Player title, and led his team to the WHA championship.
