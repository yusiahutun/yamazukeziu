US soccer star Kelley O'Hara strips down for ESPN's The Body Issue

US soccer star Kelley O'Hara stripped down to absolutely nothing for ESPN The Magazine's 2019 The Body Issue.

The 31-year-old FIFA Women's World Cup champion and Olympic gold medalist has worn quite a few uniforms over the course of her nearly decade-long professional career, but she ditched them all in favor of the skin she's in for the magazine's annual nude issue. 

Posing for one of 17 covers, Kelley shows off the muscular figure — and a butt she's quite proud of — that got her to where she is today.

Kelley — who recently won her second World Cup with the US Women's National Team — proudly flaunted her toned legs, strong arms, and impressive abs in a cover shot that shows her with one foot propped on a soccer ball and her hands holding a beer.

The 5′ 5″ stunner looks strong as ever, despite arriving at the shoot in a boot to protect the ankle injury she'd sustained the day before.

In behind-the-scenes video, she admits she's 'surprised' by how comfortable she felt the first time she took off her robe on set.

'I've always loved The Body Issue. I think it's beautiful, I think it's empowering. I just think it's such a cool display of athletes and sport in general and the human figure.'

Though she felt pretty confident at the shoot, however, she admitted that she hasn't always embraced the way her body looked.

'A lot of people take our confidence and our excitement and our celebrating as arrogant, but it's not. It's just embracing the game and the joy of playing on that stage for your country.'

She also hit back at those who don't agree with her and her teammates fighting for equal pay.

'Winning the World Cup was really important for us to continue to fight for what we believe we deserve.

'We're trying to push the sport forward, not just in this country but in the world. It's not a fun fight. It's not an easy fight. But the generations before us have done it, and we feel a responsibility to continue to push.'
