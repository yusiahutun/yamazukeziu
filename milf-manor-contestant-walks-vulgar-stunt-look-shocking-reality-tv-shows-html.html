MILF Manor: As contestant walks out over vulgar stunt - a look at other shocking reality TV shows

TLC series, MILF Manor, has quickly cemented its place as one of the most controversial reality shows to ever hit the small screen following its launch in January.

The unique dating series centers around eight bona fide 'hot moms' vying for a chance at love with much younger men.

Ranging from the ages of 40 to 60, the cast of women travel to a seaside mansion in Mexico only to discover that the young men are their Gen Z sons.

Sunday's episode saw the men perform blindfolded massages on their nearly-naked mothers, much to the horror of viewers.

They were lead into a room of the house where they found sex toys, fruits, and a board with drawings of female anatomy.

As the mother's picked the boys to answer a series of questions, it resulted in shocking scenes such as contestant Ryan W. showcasing his oral capabilities on a doll - in front of his own mother.

Meanwhile, DailyMail.com's Maureen Callahan labeled the series as the most 'repulsive, exploitative and sordid' show in reality TV history.

Playing It Straight, 2004: Male contestants hid the fact they were gay from female co-stars

Playing It Straight saw a young woman put on a ranch in Nevada with 14 men, but only five of them were straight

If the final suitor was straight, the pair would split a $1,000,000 reward. However, if the final suitor was gay then he alone would receive the reward

Fox announced the series' removal from the network's schedule due to poor ratings after just three episodes

Playing It Straight aired on Fox in 2004 and invited the audience to figure out alongside contestant Jackie which men were gay or straight.

The college student was put on a ranch in Nevada with 14 men, but only five of them were straight.

In an effort to discover which men were gay, Jackie participated in group activities and went on individual dates with all of the suitors.

At the end of each episode, Jackie was required to eliminate two suitors who she believed to be gay.

If the final suitor was a straight man, the pair would split a $1,000,000 reward, however, if the final suitor was  gay then he alone would receive the reward.

The show received generally unfavorable reviews from television critics. Dana Stevens of Slate stated: '[N]ot only is Playing It Straight ideologically offensive, it’s also colossally boring.'

Viewers appeared to feel the same way because Fox announced the series' removal from the network's schedule due to poor ratings of its three aired episodes.

The network had insisted that they intended to air the five remaining episodes at some point during the summer season, but it never happened.

An Australian version of the show aired in 2004, while a British version - hosted by actress Jameela Jamil - hit the small screen in 2005.

The winner of the competition received the wedding of her dreams, and had her entire wish list fulfilled

Travis Barker's ex Shanna Moakler was the host of the controversial E! Network reality series, Bridalplasty, in 2010.

The show featured 12 women who were competing to win a wedding and their dream plastic surgery procedure for their big day. 

Each woman had a plastic surgery wishlist, and the winner of each week's wedding-themed challenge would get to win one plastic surgery procedure from her list.

The winner of the competition received the wedding of her dreams, and had her entire wish list fulfilled - with the surgery performed by Botched star Dr. Terry Dubrow.

The husband-to-be was then not able to see his fiancée until she revealed her new look on their wedding day.

Illinois native Allyson Donovan took out New York's Jenessa Wainwright in the order to win the finale. 

Winner Allyson's list consisted of a mammoth seven surgeries. They included a brow-lift, liposuction of the chin, jowls, cheeks, a breast reduction, breast lift, tummy tuck, laser skin resurfacing and liposuction of the inner thighs, outer thighs and arms.

Before the finale, Allyson had already received liposuction on her arms and her chin as well as veneers.

The premise was widely criticized for its embrace of cosmetic surgery, with some outlets calling the show 'dangerous'.

It was also accused of 'pushing the limits of medical ethics', as the American Society of Plastic Surgeons prohibits surgeons from performing procedures as the prize for a contest. 

 

Who's Your Daddy?, 2005: Adopted women have to guess who their real father is

Each episode of Who's Your Daddy? would see a woman, who had been placed for adoption as a child, given the chance to figure out who her biological father is

The woman would have to try and find her father among a group that was also full of impostors

However, the show would see the men rewarded with money if they manage to dupe the ladies into thinking they were related

The pilot episode was the only one that made it on air after the show rubbed audiences - and adoption advocacy groups - the wrong way

Who's Your Daddy? was another Fox show that has been noted as one of the most bizarre reality shows to hit screens during the early 2000s.

Hosted by All My Children actress Finola Hughes, each episode would see a woman, who had been placed for adoption as a child, given the chance to figure out who her biological father is in a room that was also full of impostors.

However, the show would see the men rewarded with money if they manage to dupe the ladies into thinking they were related.

The contestant would win six figures if they picked correctly, but an incorrect selection would net the falsely-identified father the cash, while the contestant would still get to link up with their biological father. Gross. 

The pilot episode was the only one that made it on air, as backlash over the cruel trickery in the show rubbed audiences - and adoption advocacy groups - the wrong way.

Deborah Capone, a single mother with a 5-year-old adopted daughter, began an e-mail campaign asking for the show to be axed. 

'By turning adoption reunions into a game show, "Who’s Your Daddy?" takes an intensely personal and complex situation... and transforms it into a voyeuristic display,' Capone said, according to Today.com.

The lone episode that did air saw the contestant correctly identify her father, winning $100,000 in the process. 

 

There’s Something about Miriam, 2004: Contestants didn't know they were dating a transgender woman

There's Something about Miriam was a British reality dating series which saw six men competing to win the heart of Mexican model, Miriam Rivera

Though the audience knew that then 21-year-old Miriam was actually a transgender women, the contestants themselves did not

 The men went on to sue the creators of the show for personal injury in the form of psychological and emotional damage and settled for an unknown amount 

There's Something about Miriam was a British reality dating series which saw six men competing to win the heart of Mexican model, Miriam Rivera.

Though the audience knew that then 21-year-old Miriam was a transgender women, the contestants themselves did not. 

The men would take part in challenges so they could get the chance of going on a date with her.

Eventually, Miriam whittled the group down to her top two and picked her winner - who was then meant to whisk her away on a romantic cruise.

However, once it was revealed to the men that Miriam was transgender, the winner eventually rejected the prize money and refused to go on the cruise with her.

The men were so outraged that they went on to sue the creators of the show for conspiracy to commit sexual assault, defamation, breach of contract and personal injury in the form of psychological and emotional damage.

After causing a delay to the series airing on TV, the men settled for an amount that was not disclosed.

The program was filmed in 2003, and aired on Sky One in the UK and Channel 10 in Australia the following year amid great controversy. 

It also aired in Poland and Argentina in 2005, before being broadcast in the United States in October 2007. 

Fans were left devastated in 2019 when it was revealed that Miriam had been found dead at her home in Mexico aged 38.

Her husband Daniel Cuervo believed her death was under suspicious circumstances though it was officially recorded as a suicide.

He told Daily Mail at the time that Miriam had been the victim of attempted murders in the past. 

The story of the show was explored further in a 2021 podcast titled Harsh Reality: The Story of Miriam Rivera. 

Narrated by trans actress Trace Lysette, it delved into what was happening behind the scenes of the show and puts it into context with the reality facing transgender people at the time.

The Swan - supposedly based on the fairytale The Ugly Duckling - and saw women deemed to be unattractive and suffering from low self-esteem given extreme makeovers

In the years since it aired on Fox in 2004, The Swan has been labeled the worst reality series ever by Entertainment Weekly.

The show saw 16 'ugly duckling' women competing in the hopes that they would make it to the grand pageant final - thanks to a complete plastic surgery overhaul.

The show’s voiceover explained: 'A group of ordinary women hand over their lives to a team of cosmetic and plastic surgeons. They will be put through a brutal three month makeover all for the chance to become beauty queens. 

'Each week, two contestants will be transformed, but only will be judged beautiful enough to move on to the pageant.'  

The Swan claimed to be based on the fairytale The Ugly Duckling and saw them cast women deemed to be unattractive who were suffering from low self-esteem and gave them extreme makeovers.
