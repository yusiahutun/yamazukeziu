Pele is a popular and professional former Brazilian football player. Pele was one of the most successful and popular sports figures of the 20th century as well as regarded as one of the greatest football players of all.

Early Life and Childhood

Pele was born on October 23, 1940, in Minas Gerais, Brazil. His age is 82 years old as of 2022 and his full name is Edson Arantes do Nascimento.

Similarly, he is a naturalized Brazilian citizen and belongs to the Afro-Brazilian ethnicity. Moreover, he is Catholic by religion and his birth sign is Scorpio. He was born to Fluminense footballer Dondinho (father) and a housewife Celeste Arantes (mother).

He had two siblings brother Zeca Nascimento and a sister Maria Lucia Nascimento and he was the elder among them. Moreover, he was named after the talented American inventor Thomas Edison. He was given the nickname “Pele” during his school days, due to his pronunciation of the name of his favorite player.

Career and Professional Life

Pele started his career joining club Santos at the age of 15 and joining the Brazil national team at the age of 16. In July 1957 he played his first international game which was against Argentina. Even though Brazil lost the match by 2-1, he scored his first international goal, which made him the youngest player ever to score in international football.

In addition, he is Brazil’s all-time leading scorer, with 77 goals in 92 games. With 643 goals in 659 games for Santos, he is the club’s all-time leading scorer. Similarly, he led Santos to the Copa Libertadores in 1962 and 1963, as well as the Intercontinental Cup in 1962 and 1963.

In 1969, at the Maracana Stadium, he scored his 1000th goal against Vasco da Gama from a penalty kick. Moreover, he was the only player to win three FIFA World Cups during his international career, in 1958, 1962, and 1970.

The 1970 World Cup was the final World Cup he participated. Brazil won the World Cup naming him “Player of the Tournament” for his performance and significant contribution. Similarly, his last international match was on July 18, 1971, against Yugoslavia in Rio de Janeiro. Before retiring, the 1974 season was the 19th and last season that he played for his club Santos.

Furthermore, he was UNESCO Goodwill Ambassador in 1995, and in 1999, he was named “Athlete of the Century” by the International Olympic Committee. Similarly, he was named World Player of the Century by the International Federation of Football History & Statistics in 2000. He was also named FIFA Player of the Century in 2000.

More about his career

In addition, his 1,279 goals in 1,363 games, including friendlies, is a Guinness World Record. Additionally, he was a worldwide ambassador for football and did numerous acting and commercial ventures since retiring in 1977. Further, he was honored as president of the New York Cosmos in 2010.

Also, he is regarded as a national hero in Brazil for his football accomplishments as well as his outspoken support for policies that improve the social conditions of the poor people.

Moreover, throughout his career and after retiring, he received numerous individual and team awards for his on-field performance, record-breaking achievements, and legacy in the sport.

Achievements
