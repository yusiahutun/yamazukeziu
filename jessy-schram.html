American actress, Jessy Schram is best known for her role in the TV series, ‘Falling Skies.’ She is also a model who has modeled for various high-end commercials and brands. Her other notable works include ‘Last Resort’, ‘The Lizzie Borden Chronicles’, and ‘Nashville.’ Schram earned popularity after portraying Tracy on American Pie Presents: The Naked Mile. She is currently single and has been involved in any relationships in the past.

Early Life and Education

Jessy Schram was born Jessica Schram on 15th January 1986 in Skokie, Illinois, USA. She belongs to white-American ethnicity and holds American nationality.

Schram is the daughter born to Kristie Schram and John Schram. She has two siblings, Nicole Schram and Jonathan Schram. She started modeling at the age of 12. She was recognized by the Stewart Talent Agency in Chicago in 1996. She also appeared in TV commercials and began performing in musical theatre. 

Schram joined Buffalo Grove High School; graduated in 2004. Later, she moved to Los Angeles to pursue her acting career.  She has worked in two commercials for Retin-A Micro and made her TV debut on August 5, 2002.

Career

Jessy Schram first appeared in an episode of American television sitcom, Drake & Josh in 2004. The next year, she worked in mystery films series, Jane Doe as Susan Davis. She appeared in five episodes; Jane Doe: Vanishing Act, Jane Doe: Now You See It, Now You Don't, Jane Doe: Til Death Do Us Part and Jane Doe: The Wrong Face.

In 2006, Schram was cast in the movies such as I Want Someone to Eat Cheese With as Fake Daughter and American Pie Presents: The Naked Mile as Tracy Sterling. In American Pie Presents: The Naked Mile, she worked with Melanie Merkosky.

In 2005, Schram got featured in American television drama series, Medium. She acted in mystery films series, Jane Doe in 2006. She was cast in two episode; Jane Doe: The Harder They Fall and Jane Doe: Yes, I Remember It Well.

Schram played the role of Tracy in 2006 sex comedy movie, American Pie Presents: The Naked Mile. She starred alongside John White, Steve Talley, and Christopher McDonald, among others.

In 2007, Schram worked in four movies; House, Ghost Whisperer, Without a Trace, CSI: Miami, Jane Doe: Ties That Bind and Jane Doe: How to Fire Your Boss.

Schram played the role of Rachel Seybolt in an American crime drama television program, Life in 2007. The following year, she guest-starred on the shows Ghost Whisperer, Without a Trace, House, and CSI: Miami.

Schram appeared in an American independent drama film, Keith in 2008. She portrayed Courtney in the film.

In the year 2010, she was also cast in an American disaster thriller film, Unstoppable and also in the TV movies, Betwixt and Night and Day. The following year, Shram appeared in a mystery-drama series, The Mentalist: starring Simon Baker, Robin Tunney, Tim Kang, and Owain Yeoman, among others.

The American actress starred in American science fiction TV series, Falling Skies in 2011. She also played Ashley Boyd/Cinderella in the show Once Upon a Time, starring Ginnifer Goodwin, Jennifer Morrison, Lana Parrilla, and Josh Dallas among others.

Schram played a lead role in American military drama series, Last Resort, alongside Andre Braugher, Scott Speedman, Daisy Betts, and Camille De Pazzis among others. She appeared in four episodes of Mad Men in 2014.

In 2015, the American actress played in the movie The Submarine Kid and in the drama The Lizzie Borden Chronicles. The following year, she played in Harvest Moon and Reluctant Nanny.

Schram had a recurring role in the episodes of American musical drama television series, Nashville, with Connie Britton, Hayden Panettiere, Clare Bowen, and Eric Close, among others.

Schram’s recent appearance on television includes 2017 film Birthday Wish, A Royal New Year's Eve, and The Birthday Wish. She also appeared in KFC commercials and also featured in a music video of J.R. Richards' song, "A Beautiful End."

Personal Life

Jessy Schram has been a natural performer since her early childhood days. Her talent was recognized at the age of 10. She established herself as one of the most successful child models in Chicago by booking numerous commercials, print campaigns, voice-overs, and television work.

Schram is unmarried and has no children. She was linked to an Albanian-American actor, Victor Gojcaj. They worked together in the thriller film, Unstoppable. It was also believed that the two got close to each other during the filming the movie, as per some online sources.

In addition, Schram has performed as a singer/songwriter, and solo artist. She toured with Joan Baby's soul band, and perform at places like The Knitting Factory, Hard Rock Cafe, Tweeter Center, Soldier Field, Navy Pier, Hancock Music Center, and many more.

Jessy continues to learn guitar and explore percussion developing her unique style. She Is a big fan of The Rolling Stones.
