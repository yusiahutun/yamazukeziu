Quadriplegic man Joel Jackson is hoping to start a family with his new bride

Most newly wed couples only have to worry about predictable issues - making more time for each other or planning for a family.

But Lauren and Joel Jackson have much bigger concerns. Joel is a quadriplegic who, by most accounts, shouldn’t even be alive. 

In 2009, Joel was 'internally decapitated' after riding in a car with a drunk driver who crashed into a telephone pole, ‘throwing him nearly half a football field,’ Lauren told the DailyMail.com in an exclusive interview. 

The driver, who was 29 at the time, was charged with a felony DUI, driving with a suspended license and child endangerment. A 15-year-old girl in the car was killed in the crash.

The accident severed Joel's spine from his skull, paralyzing him from the neck down with almost no chance of survival.

Doctors called the organ donation team and told Joel's family to start planning a funeral. He lost a section of spine, suffered a twice-broken jaw, a broken pelvis and brain stem damage.

He spent 87 days in a coma and was finally released a week before his 21st birthday.

During the ordeal, Lauren became a regular visitor at his bedside - they’d met as teenagers at a church youth event and briefly dated years earlier - although Joel could only communicate by looking up for ‘yes’ and down for ‘no.’

'Joel learned to pucker [for a kiss] as soon as he came home from the hospital. He worked with a speech therapist for a short time to help him learn to do that,’ Lauren told the DailyMail.com.

‘Joel can move his head and his lips and kiss me just like any other couple.

'He has a very limited range of motion, but it has only improved. He can move his arms and hands and he can rub my hand with his thumb,' she said.

Joel can also interact using a computer that tracks his eye movements, allowing him to communicate in a similar way as Stephen Hawking. He can control the TV, send emails and texts and use a computer. 

Lauren and Joel, both 27, started dating in December 2012 and were married ten months later.

The couple became an internet sensation after posting a video to their blog of themselves dancing to their wedding song, Edwin McCain's 'I'll Be,' at a rehabilitation hospital in Jacksonville, Florida. 

And now they are planning to start a family.

Lauren reveals that she and Joel enjoy an active sex life, calling it the ‘most special’ she’s ever had. It was their wedding night when the couple had sex for the first time.

‘We are planning to have a family the all-natural way. Sex is special for Joel and me because of the bond we share.

'We know each other better than we know ourselves, and when you feel as comfortable with each other as we do, it makes it more intimate and sweet.

'We waited until our wedding night to have sex for the first time together, something we wanted to honor God with for our marriage.

'And it was just that: beautiful and highly anticipated.

‘It isn't always hot and steamy, like in the movies, but it is simply our love for each other that makes it the best we have ever had.

'Sharing a bond that makes two become one - physically, sexually, intellectually, emotionally, and spiritually - is the best gift God gave us on this side of Heaven, after Jesus of course!'

But their intimacy still must be planned with military precision; Joel’s medicine and nutrition has to be controlled for him to be able to ‘perform’. 

‘He has complete feeling and everything works down there - most of the time. With that being said, everything physically and intimately, has to be completely coordinated and we can't be spontaneous.

'Not only do some of his medications cause erectile dysfunction, even Aleve, Ibuprofen, or Advil interferes. His schedule is not as flexible as most, and even the smallest things, like not being hydrated enough, can all cause issues sexually.

‘We have to plan nearly every part of our day, which leaves very little room for spontaneity. If we cannot have kids for fertility reasons alone, we will most likely adopt.

'But from a physical and sexual stand point, he can father a child just like anyone who isn't paralyzed.

‘Joel is there for me in ways no one else is. I'm not attracted to someone because of what they can do for me or because of some ability. I'm attracted to Joel because of who he is and how he supports me emotionally.

'He is inspirational and loving. Some may never understand that, and that's okay for them. When I'm going through something, Joel knows just what words I need to help me overcome it. 

'Sex isn't everything. We enjoy each other. Period. We spend 24 hours a day, seven days a week together. That's what we have chosen and that's what works for us. We never get sick of each other.’

Joel's incredible recovery is thanks to a revolutionary gymnasium, including a $15,000 exercise bike, which enabled him to first be learn to stroke Lauren’s hand and kiss her. 

In 2014, the couple moved from their home town in South Carolina to Brooks, Florida, where Joel can get more specialized care at the local neurology recovery center.

In 2014, the couple moved from their home town in South Carolina to Brooks, Florida to be closer to the neurology recovery center

'Joel has gained 20lbs of muscle mass, he feels better. His neck muscles are now strong enough to support his head on his own,' Lauren said

‘He was never offered rehab or therapy of this magnitude [in South Carolina] whether for insurance reasons or their lack of knowledge of Joel's injury level, which very few have seen alive post-injury,' Lauren said.

‘After waiting 13 months of searching and referrals from one city to the next we found Brooks in Jacksonville.

'Since we started going to Brooks Rehabilitation three days per week, Joel has gained 20lbs of muscle mass, he feels better. His neck muscles are now strong enough to support his head on his own.

‘He has used other equipment but this [the bike] is by far his favorite. The FES Bike uses electrical stimulation to activate the muscles to work along with the workout from the bike.

Joel creates his unique works by holding a paint brush in his mouth, and the couple hopes they can open a wedding venue with the money they raise

To raise money for his rehabilitation, the couple has set up an Etsy shop where they sell artwork they created together

'It is customized for Joel's tolerance and muscle groups. It also has the ability to be used the same way for his arms. These bikes cost roughly $15,000,’ she said.

The couple even decided to forgo Christmas presents this year to save up enough money for the bike and begin using it at home.

Lauren added: ‘Joel and I didn't get each other anything for Christmas - we are saving every penny ourselves to grow our businesses and pay for this bike.

'In being proactive, Joel knows that I cannot physically take him to therapy when we have young children to care for, so he wants to be able to workout from home.’

The pair has been scrimping on the small allowance Lauren receives to care for Joel, and even lived with his family in the first two and a half years of marriage.

But now they’re determined to live more independently. When their story hit the headlines, they set up a website and asked for donations in return for artwork designed by Joel.

The budding entrepreneurs also set up an Etsy store online, selling products that they worked on together, with Joel able to paint and design using his mouth. 

‘He paints anything from seaside creatures to nursery art for babies. I have a background in art and design so I started drawing things to see if he could paint them. Sure enough, over time, he got better and better.

‘He has painted by holding a paint brush in his mouth for the last two years and is now putting all funds he acquires from his work into his bike fund, so he doesn't lose the momentum he’s gained from going to therapy three days per week,' she said.

Lauren is also hoping to make her own professional dreams come true, especially so she can be closer to Joel if he needs her.

The money they've raised has been enough to put a deposit on a five-acre farmhouse exactly a year ago, and the pair are in the process of converting it into a wedding venue. 

‘I have been a wedding photographer and coordinator since 2012. I had always wanted to own a wedding venue where I could work from home.

'This coming spring we plan to open our doors and provide couples with an all-inclusive wedding venue. We have just finished construction on our bridal cottage.

‘I’ll be able to care for Joel and financially provide for our hopefully growing family. We’ll no longer qualify to receive help for his care through insurance once we get started, but our plans are to be independent and self-sufficient anyway,' she said.

In the end, the couple is merely thankful that they found each other. 

‘He is the best person for advice because he truly cares for the people in his life and he gives it to you honest. Sometimes it’s as simple as a wink or a smile.

'Most of the time, we know each other so well, we know what the other is thinking without having to speak,' Lauren said.
