2 coin worth THOUSANDS and it could be in YOUR pocket

The 2007 Act of Union coin is one of a handful for sale on eBay with an asking price of thousands.

Produced in 2007, the coin was made to celebrate 300 years since Scotland and England united under one Parliament in 1907.

Divided into four quarters, the design features a rose and a thistle occupying two of the quarters to represent Scotland, and a portcullis which represents the Houses of Parliament on the other two.

More than 7.5 million of the limited edition coins were made, but it's not known how many are still in circulation.

Two further coins are on sale for around £2,000, one of which comes in a sealed box

Meanwhile another such coin, described as in “very good condition”, is on sale at a Buy It Now price of £1,750, and is open to best offers.

And another, priced at £1,699.95, even comes with a Royal Mint Boxed With Certificate of Authenticity.

The coin, which was designed by Yvonne Holton, also features the dates 1707 and 2007, while the Queen's head is adorned on the reverse.

Describing the history of the coin, The Royal Mint website writes: “The design divides the coin into four quarters, with a rose and a thistle occupying two of the quarters and a portcullis in each of the other two quarters.

“The whole design is overlaid with a linking jigsaw motif and surrounded by the dates '1707', '2007' and the denomination 'TWO POUNDS'.

"When James VI of Scotland became James I of England in 1603, the two countries were united under one monarchy.

"Over the course of the seventeenth century, there were several attempts to bring about a closer union between the two countries but more than a century passed before Scotland and England were finally united under one Parliament.

"The Act was passed in 1707 and to commemorate the 300th anniversary of a remarkable union, The Royal Mint issued a special £2 coin."

Sadly, the seller is unlikely to sell the coin for the amount listed.

According to ChangeChecker.org the coin usually sells for around £2.50 to £2.80 on eBay.

The coin experts put together a “scarcity” index for coins to indicate the rarest and most sought after space change.

Daily Star Online previously revealed the rare £2 coins in circulation.

The rarest – the 2002 Commonwealth Games Northern Ireland coin – has recently sold or between £35 and £50.
