Lana Del Rey Speaks on Awkward Moment With Taylor Swift at the 2024 Grammys

A lot of people weren’t pleased with Taylor Swift at the 66th Grammy Awards ceremony last Sunday. The singer made history when she won the coveted Album of the Year award—which made her the only artist in Grammy history to win in that category four times—but her way of accepting the golden gramophone had a lot of viewers raising eyebrows.

Besides seeming to ignore Céline Dion, who made a surprise appearance (despite her illness) that evening to present the award, Swift also drew criticism from fans for bringing pal Lana Del Rey onstage for her Album of the Year acceptance speech. Del Rey appeared on “Snow on the Beach,” a track from Swift’s winning album, Midnights—but she herself was also a nominee in the category, for her ninth studio album, Did You Know That There’s A Tunnel Under Ocean Blvd.

John Shearer//Getty Images

The decision quickly snowballed into controversy on social media. In one viral tweet, a user wrote, “Taylor dragged Lana (who just lost the award) on stage when she CLEARLY didn’t want to go, and then didn’t say a WORD to Celine fucking Dion when she took the award?”

On Wednesday, however, Del Rey officially set the record straight, leaving a comment on a fan account’s Instagram post, and shutting down rumors of bad blood between herself and Swift.

“I literally just loved being there,” she wrote of her Grammys experience, in a screenshot published by People. “I love to see everyone, get the tea and hang out. Get dressed up.”

She added, “I literally did not feel 1 ounce of negative emotion at any point in that award ceremony. It was pure funniness and laughter.”

After Sunday night’s ceremony, Del Rey—who was nominated in five categories but ultimately went home with no Grammys—continued to celebrate Swift’s win by sharing a video of the moment it was announced on Instagram. No bitterness detected here.

And in case you were wondering how things really went down with Dion, the two squashed the snub allegations with affectionate photos taken backstage shortly after Swift’s acceptance speech.
