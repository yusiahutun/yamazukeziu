Les Prs d'Eugnie, Gascony, France

Picture: the spa, courtesy of Les Prés d'Eugénie

Eugénie-les-Bains, a sleepy town in south-west France, has all the necessary credentials for a first-rate spa resort (thermal waters, secluded valley setting, diet-friendly food), but in Michel Guérard’s Les Prés d’Eugénie it has a stellar one. Feast on three-Michelin-starred food, or opt for the equally tasty low-calorie option; settle for sundowners by the palm-trees on the lawn or on a sunlounger by the pool; and while away hours in the charming traditional spa as the rich waters soothe you. The Relais & Châteaux retreat is just over a 100 miles south of Bordeaux and west of Toulouse, in the heart of Aquitaine.
 
Accomodation
The Suites Royale in the main house are especially lavish: we loved the sloped ceilings and chandelier in L’Impératrice; the namesake Les Prés d’Eugénie is especially huge and decadent. Ornate bathrooms, dripping in gold, marble and space, come as standard in all rooms. The famous chef behind the resort is the town’s golden boy and he has gradually taken over various parts of the tiny commune, changing them into more accommodation for his spa retreat. He has also set up a cookery school.
 
Wow factor
Sit down for a three-course Grande Cuisine Minceur lunch and you won’t believe how few calories you’ve consumed. Michel Guérard’s show-stopping ‘slimming cuisine’ allows you to eat beef salad, ox cheek with couscous and mango (below), and berries with lime sorbet while keeping to the constraints of any diet. As you can expect for a chef who has held three Michelin stars for the best part of four decades, the taste is not affected.
 

Picture: courtesy of Caroline Lewis

Eating and drinking
If you want to lose weight, you won’t find diet food more palate-friendly than the specialist Cuisine Minceur on offer here, but it would be impolite to pass up on a tasting menu (of varying lengths) come evening. After an aperitif in the lounge, amid treasures from the Guérards’ travels, retire to the dining-room for an opulent feast: pick your own langoustine from a gleaming silver tray, eat tender wood-smoked fillet of beef, but save some room for the trolley of cheese circling the room and a taste of the chef’s sublime berry-topped pastry. There’s also another restaurant in the town, Auberge de la Ferme aux Grives, where guests can try a more rustic take on classic Gascon cuisine.
 
Spa
There is a municipal spa nearby, set up by the French government to utilise the healing thermal waters of Eugénie-les-Bains and to offer rest and recuperation to stressed or overweight public-sector workers – but Les Prés d’Eugénie’s offering is a world apart. Set in a quaint wooden farmhouse with hanging baskets for your belongings, potted herbs adorning the lounge and old-fashioned button-down dressing-gowns to add yet more antiquated charm, the spa offers thermal treatments including a float in a clay-filled bath and a hosing down by cellulite-targeting hydrology jets. Eugénie-les-Bains has been known for its healing waters since the Empress Eugénie, wife of Napoleon III, gave her name to the town in 1861.
 
Book it
Les Prés d’Eugénie, 234 Rue René Vielle, Eugénie-les-Bains, Landes, France, from about £195 a room a night (+33 5 58 05 06 07). The hotel is roughly a 90-minute drive south from Bordeaux Airport.
