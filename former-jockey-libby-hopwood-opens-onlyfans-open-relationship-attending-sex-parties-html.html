Former jockey Libby Hopwood opens up about OnlyFans, her open relationship and attending sex parties

Jockey-turned-OnlyFans creator Libby Hopwood has revealed she's an exhibitionist at heart which lead her to her new career in the adult industry.

Speaking on Samantha X's podcast, Xposed, Libby also revealed her disappointment at being 'outed' to her former colleagues who had very mixed reactions about her career choice.

'I've got the support of my partner and I've been more open about it this time. It's bit of a dynamic shift but I'm excited about it and happy to put it out there.'

Libby dove in knowing little about the platform or anyone else who was making a living off it but quickly racked up a throng of fans. 

She wasn't ready to attach her identity to her OnlyFans alter ego so called herself Foxy Miss, a name she still uses today, however some close friends knew about her saucy side hustle.  

'In our little kinky world, I love wearing foxes and a fox tail. I feel adorable dressed as a fox. I also like how feral and playful foxes I speak to me on a human level,' she said.

However, the face behind Foxy Miss was exposed after someone leaked her private content within the racing community. 

'We were actually in Europe on a holiday and somebody sent me a Snapchat along the lines of 'Can I get a freebie?'' she recalled. 

'I messaged my friend who was back in Sydney I was like 'I think my OnlyFans has blown up', she's like 'I actually wanted to wait until you're back in Sydney before I spoke to you about it, I didn't want to ruin your holiday but it's everywhere'.' 

The images and footage spread like wildfire among everyone Libby knew and she said she was getting 'a million messages'. 

'There was nobody in racing doing all OnlyFans and I knew there was going to be a stigma around that because racing is a dinosaurs game,' she said.

While many people have been supportive of Libby's new lucrative career, she has been met with backlash even with colleagues she worked with for years.

'One of my trainers who I have a great deal of respect for and I've worked for him and my other role with horses for years, had a quite a visceral reaction,' she said.

'He was like, 'What the hell are you doing?' and threw his hands up, walked off and sort of didn't talk to me.'

She wasn't surprised by the negative reactions as racing has a lot of 'old school' and 'conservative' characters. 

'People always are lashing out, especially against our bodies and our sexuality because that is the most weaponised thing against women...It's low hanging fruit, it's easy to take a shot at it,' Libby said. 

'I don't care what they have to think. The work that they need to do to become a better person to be nicer in the universe, they're not going to do that so there's no point me getting upset about what they want to spew back at me.'
