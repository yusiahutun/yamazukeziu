TikToker Nyako Threatens to Sue Safaricom for Making Public Her Finance Records

  • Nyako reiterated that she was a content creator of international acclaim and was entitled to the right to privacy
  • She accused Safaricom of illegally making her financial information accessible to third parties without her consent
  • The content creator has given the telco seven days to apologise and agree to an out-of-court agreement, failure to which she would institute a legal suit

TikTok sensation Roseline Otieno, alias Nyako, has threatened to sue giant telco Safaricom for allegedly making public her financial transactions without her consent.

Nyako threatens to sue Safaricom

She accuses the firm of making her M-Pesa transactions visible to third parties.

In a demand letter through the law firm of Danstan Omari Associates and Advocates, Nyako says that the telco allowed the circulation of confidential details of her M-Pesa statements on social media platforms.

"We act for Roseline Otieno, who goes as 'Nyako' on TikTok, under whose instructions we address you as follows: that on or about February 13, 2024, you disseminated or facilitated the dissemination of her confidential information to unauthorised individuals and have been circulating on various social media platforms," the letter reads.

Nyako claimed that the company released crucial details of her financial transaction history to strangers.

What is an M-Pesa statement?

"Our client's M-Pesa statements, containing her personal details and financial transaction history, a dataset exclusively maintained by your organisation, were released to unauthorised persons and have been circulating on various social media platforms," continued the letter seen by TUKO.co.ke.

Nyako wants Safaricom to admit liability for leaking her M-Pesa details, after which her lawyer will quantify damages.

"That you admit liability for breaching our client’s right to privacy, which thereafter we shall quantify damages," her advocates state in the letter.

The German-based TikToker claimed that the disclosure of her M-Pesa details had caused her tremendous anxiety and undermined her brand.

Privacy rights in Kenya

"Consequently, the unauthorised disclosure of her personal and financial information to the public, particularly to unauthorised individuals, compromises her privacy rights, undermines her standing as a reputable brand, and exposes her to financial risks. This has also inflicted emotional distress and anxiety upon our client," the lawyers reiterated.

She also issued a seven-day ultimatum to the company to explain how her confidential information was disseminated to the public. If the company does not comply, she will institute legal proceedings.

"That there be an immediate explanation for the unauthorised dissemination of our client's confidential information. Take note that failure to comply with the above demands within seven days of the date hereof, we have firm and irrevocable," Nyako demanded.

Her advocate censured the firm for insisting it had the duty to protect sensitive information belonging to their subscribers.

Mobile service providers' protection of clients' information

"As custodian of this sensitive data, your organisation has the responsibility to safeguard it against unauthorised disclosure and misuse," Omari stated.

Omari accused the company of infringing on her right to privacy and breaching the data protection law.

"Unauthorised disclosure of personal data and granting access to unauthorized persons which constitutes a clear personal data breach as defined in Section 2 of the Data Protection Act," Omari added.

Nyako, who said on TikTok that she was looking for a good lawyer, added that she was a renowned content creator of international acclaim.

"Our client is a renowned content creator of international acclaim, commands significant recognition and embodies a global brand. Her standing in the industry places her in direct competition with entities of considerable stature," her lawyers affirmed.

Why Nyako's bank account was closed

In February, Nyako expressed her frustration that German authorities had frozen her bank accounts.

She accused her detractors of maliciously reporting her to the authorities for reasons she had yet to find out.

The content creator said she has been making a living from TikTok since losing her job in 2023.

She said that she used to receive gifts from her fans whenever she went live on the app, citing it as her main source of income.

"My bank account has been blocked. I cannot access it. It has been blocked. I don’t know who wrote what again to the government this time. I have to pay my rent using gifts from TikTok. I have to pay my insurance from Tick, Tock. That money has been taken away. I don’t even know where to start at this point. I don’t know who to ask what," she lamented.

The telco's communications person said the firm would confirm receipt of the demand letter and respond appropriately thereafter.
