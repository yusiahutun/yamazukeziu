Jane Austen quotes - love, marriage and money

Getty Images

Two hundred years after her death, Jane Austen still hasn't lost her appeal. Her books are still widely read and characters such as Elizabeth Bennett, Mr Darcy, Anne Elliott and Elinor Dashwood are ever ingrained in the mind's eye.

We look at the world according to Austen, as told through her literature.

Reading is crucial

"The person, be it gentleman or lady, who has not pleasure in a good novel, must be intolerably stupid." - Northanger Abbey

Love doesn't always look like what you think it will

"There are as many forms of love as there are moments in time."

Actions matter, not words

"It isn't what we say or think that defines us, but what we do." - Sense & Sensibility

Have faith

"Know your own happiness. Want for nothing but patience - or give it a more fascinating name: Call it hope." - Sense and Sensibility

Never let happiness pass you by

"Why not seize pleasure at once? How often is happiness destroyed by preparation, foolish preparation!" - Emma

Remember that marriage is not easy

"Happiness in marriage is entirely by chance." - Pride & Prejudice

Hot weather presents style woes for everyone

"What dreadful hot weather we have! It keeps one in a continual state of inelegance." - Jane Austen, letter, 18 September,1796

Create your own future

"If adventures will not befall a young lady in her own village, she must seek them abroad." - Northanger Abbey

There's nothing better for heartache than friends

"Friendship is certainly the finest balm for the pangs of disappointed love." - Northanger Abbey

Small talk is never fun

"Another stupid party last night; perhaps if larger they might be less intolerable, but here there were only just enough to make one card-table, with six people to look on and talk nonsense to each other." - letter to Cassandra Austen, 12 May 1801

Money does help

"A large income is the best recipe for happiness I ever heard of." - Mansfield Park

Dance

"To be fond of dancing was a certain step towards falling in love."- Pride and Prejudice

You deserve the best

"I always deserve the best treatment because I never put up with any other." - Emma

Get out of London every now and again

"The truth is, that in London it is always a sickly season. Nobody is healthy in London, nobody can be." - Emma

Don't dwell on the past

"Think only of the past as its remembrance give you pleasure" - Pride and Prejudice

Never give up

"If one scheme of happiness fails, human nature turns to another; if the first calculation is wrong, we make a second better: we find comfort somewhere." - Mansfield Park
