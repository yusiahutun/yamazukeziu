Sydney teen Ruel set for world musical domination

It's a truism of popular music that it has always been a dominion of the young.

But, after the likes of Justin Bieber and Australian Cody Simpson were discovered performing covers on YouTube as precocious teenagers, record companies have been turning to teens more frequently for the 'next big thing.'

One of those earmarked for future musical world domination is Sydney teenager Ruel.

Rising star: Prodigious Sydney teenager Ruel has been earmarked for future wworld musical domination

The 14-year-old Sydney prodigy was 'discovered' by Australian Grammy award winning producer M-Phazes after he heard the young talent cover James Bay's Let It Go in 2015.

Their collaboration on the Triple J cover's segment Like A Version, that saw the pair take on Jack Garratt's hit Weathered, has amassed more than 230,000 views on YouTube. 

Meanwhile the Herald Sun reported that his debut Don't Tell me single had raked in more than 700,000 streams since its release two weeks ago. 

Plucked from obscurity: The 14-year-old Sydney prodigy (r) was 'discovered' by Australian Grammy award winning producer M-Phazes (l) after he heard the young talent cover James Bay's Let It Go in 2015. Also pictured is Triple J group music director Richard Kingsmill

Speaking to the Herald sun, M-Phazes (aka Mark Landon) said that he was blown away by Ruel's beyond-his-years talent.

'He recorded that when he was 12 years old,' he said. 'He makes me question what I have done with my life.'

Mark went on to reveal that while Ruel's talent was undeniable, he believed it was important not to push the youngster too early.

'He recorded that when he was 12 years old,' he said. 'He makes me question what I have done with my life,' M-Phazes told the Herald Sun

'When we first heard him, we didn't know it was a young boy,' he said. We started to cultivate a relationship with him and his family and then it was really important to nurture him and not push him.'

'He is definitely in it for the long game and with an artist like Ruel who blows you away the first time you hear him, there’s no other way to go,' Mark added.

Ruel is set to join the likes of Cody Simpson and Troye Sivan who both made their mark on the musical world at young ages.

Sensation: Cody, who took to YouTube to share covers of the likes of Justin Timberlake's Señorita was discovered by Grammy-nominated producer Shawn Campbell before signing to Atlantic Records at just 13 years of age

Cody, 20, who took to YouTube to share covers of the likes of Justin Timberlake's Señorita was discovered by Grammy-nominated producer Shawn Campbell before signing to Atlantic Records at just 13 years of age.

Now based in the US Cody also runs his own label, Coast House Records.

Perth-based Troye Sivian, 22, meanwhile, first came to attention as a vlogger on YouTube has parlayed his 4,431,000-plus subscribers into a musical career that has seen him nab top ten chart spots both here and in the US.
