Who is Jaswant Singh Rai?

Jaswant is a member of the Rai family, one of the richest in Kenya, with interests in agriculture, timber, cement, edibles oils, real estate and horticulture, NTV Kenya reported.

Rai family's fortunes are traced to their patriarch, Tarlochan Singh Rai, who died on December 28, 2010, leaving Jaswant as the chairman of the Rai Group and executor of his will.

Tarlochan, who hailed from India, made forays in the Democratic Republic of Congo (DRC) by purchasing tea and coffee estates left behind by Belgians after colonisation.

He, alongside his siblings, also owned Rai Agricultural Estates in Kenya but sold the company in 1969.

In 1971, Tarlochan and his sons Jaswant, Jasbir, Sarbjit and Iqbal Rai incorporated RaiPly Kenya, which deals with timber products. Their empire has expanded since then.

He rose to fame after acquiring Webuye Paper Mills, renamed Rai Paper Company, at KSh 900 million.

Which businesses are under Rai Group?

According to Daily Nation, four firms owned by Rai account for nearly half of the total sugar sold in Kenya.

Rai has spread his wings in Western Kenya and South Nyanza, controlling major sugar belts.

He is into cement production through Rai Cement.

West Kenya, Sukari and Olepito Industries

West Kenya Sugar Factory produces the Kabras sugar brand.

According to its LinkedIn profile, the company in Kakamega county was founded in 1971. It crushes over 5,000 tonnes of cane daily and employs over 1,000 people.

Besides West Kenya, Rai owns Naitiri, Olepito and Sukari Industries.

Tulip Properties

Jaswant is the director of Tulip Properties, which has been mentioned in land scandals.

In 2020, the real estate company battled for the ownership of KSh 1.6 billion 16-acre parcel of land with other individuals, namely Simon Kiprono Laboso, Noor Mohammed Hassan, Messrs Mohammed, Koriow Nur and MacDonald Makaka.

Jaswant claimed he bought the land from Kenya's second president, Daniel Moi.

RaiPly Kenya and Timsales

The family controls the timber business in Kenya through RaiPly Kenya and a stake in Timsales associated with the Kenyatta family.

RaiPly was one of the companies that bagged tenders to supply wooden poles to the Kenya Power and Lighting Company (KPLC). Its tender was valued at KSh 112.87 million.

Timsales manufactures plywood, fibreboards, block boards, flush doors and timber, while RaiPly makes chipboards, ceilings, block boards, parquet, wooden tiles and polythene bags for sugar companies.

Rai is also behind Menengai Oil Refineries, which manufactures cooking oil, fats, soaps, detergents, baking powder and skin care products.

Who owns Sarrai Group?

Jaswant's brother, Sarbjit Singh Rai, is the tycoon behind Uganda's Sarrai Group, which kickstarted operations in Mumias Sugar Company.

Sarrai Group is one of the largest producers of sugar in the East African region, with subsidiaries in Kenya, Uganda and Malawi

In Uganda, it runs Hoima Sugar Limited, Kiryandongo Sugar Factory and Kinyara Sugar Works with a stake valued at over KSh 3.7 billion, according to Business Daily.

In December 2021, Sarrai Group secured a 20-year lease deal to manage and revive Mumias Sugar Company after it collapsed. The miller's administrator, Ponangipalli Venkata Ramana Rao, said the company won the bid due to a good track record of sugar production in Uganda.
