Who is Samantha Taffer?

Born in 1989, Samantha Taffer the offspring of the renowned American businessman and reality TV personality Jon Taffer known prominently for his role in hosting the Paramount Network’s Bar Rescue has carved out her own distinguished path. Alongside her role as co-owner and general manager of Blind Tiger a celebrated gastropub in Las Vegas Nevada Samantha’s reputation precedes her.

Having graduated from the esteemed University of Southern California she embarked on her hospitality journey in 2000. Serving as a hostess at the Palms Casino Resort in Las Vegas in her initial position, she laid the foundation for her later success.

Her expertise grew as she took on management positions at several different restaurants in Las Vegas, and then in 2014, she teamed up with her husband, Matt Hanley, to open Blind Tiger.

Her reach goes well beyond just her business activities. She’s an active member of both the Nevada Restaurant Association and the Las Vegas Chamber of Commerce.

But her caring nature doesn’t stop there. She’s a founding member of the Las Vegas Pink Ribbon Girls, a non-profit focused on breast cancer research fundraising. Her dedication to this cause shows her commitment to positively impacting others.

In recognition of her professional excellence and public service the Las Vegas Review-Journal honored her as one of the Top 40 Under 40 in 2016.

Her presence is often requested on television shows including her father’s Bar Rescue as well as on platforms like The View and The Tonight Show Starring Jimmy Fallon.

Her profile paints the picture of a successful businesswoman and a fervent philanthropist. Her contributions to the hospitality industry and her exemplary efforts in supporting breast cancer research serve as an inspiring testament to her character.

Her story stands as a beacon of inspiration and a role model for women striving in the hospitality field and those courageously battling breast cancer.
