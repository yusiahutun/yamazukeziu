Rubens Barrichello began his Formula One career in 1993.

At the young age of 20, Rubens Barrichello made his debut in the Formula One World Championship, driving for the Jordan Grand Prix team. It was the start of an illustrious career that would span over two decades.

He holds the record for the most Formula One starts.

Throughout his career, Rubens Barrichello competed in a staggering 322 Grand Prix races, earning him the title of the driver with the highest number of starts in the history of Formula One. His longevity and consistency are a testament to his skill and dedication.

Rubens Barrichello achieved 11 Grand Prix victories.

Over the course of his career, Barrichello stood on the top step of the podium 11 times, securing victories at prestigious races such as the German Grand Prix, the Italian Grand Prix, and the British Grand Prix. His exceptional driving ability and strategic decision-making played a crucial role in these successes.

He finished second in the Drivers’ Championship twice.

In the 2002 and 2004 seasons, Rubens Barrichello came agonizingly close to clinching the world title, finishing as the runner-up in the Drivers’ Championship both times. His performances were a testament to his talent and showcased his ability to consistently perform at the highest level.

Rubens Barrichello is known for his close friendship with Michael Schumacher.

During his time at Ferrari, Rubens Barrichello developed a strong bond with his teammate Michael Schumacher. The two drivers formed a remarkable partnership, with Barrichello supporting Schumacher’s title bids while also displaying his own impressive skills behind the wheel.

He competed in Formula One for 19 seasons.

Rubens Barrichello’s remarkable career spanned nearly two decades, as he continued to compete at the highest level until the end of the 2011 season. His longevity in the sport is a testament to his passion, dedication, and love for racing.

Barrichello’s son, Eduardo, is also pursuing a career in motorsports.

The Barrichello family’s racing legacy continues, as Rubens’ son, Eduardo, follows in his father’s footsteps, aiming to make a name for himself in the world of motorsports. The young talent has shown promising skills and hopes to carry on the family tradition of racing excellence.

Barrichello wore a special helmet design to honor his mother.

He holds the record for the longest gap between race wins.

In the 2009 season, Rubens Barrichello achieved his final Formula One victory at the Italian Grand Prix, which marked a remarkable 88-race gap between his last win and this one. This record stands as a testament to his perseverance and ability to remain competitive even in challenging times.

Rubens Barrichello has experienced numerous high-speed crashes but never suffered any serious injuries.

Throughout his career, Barrichello encountered several heart-stopping crashes, but miraculously, he managed to escape without sustaining any major injuries. His resilience and the safety advancements in Formula One played key roles in ensuring his well-being on the track.

Barrichello started karting at a young age.

Rubens Barrichello’s journey in motorsports began at a tender age when he first took up karting. His early experiences laid the foundation for his successful career and instilled in him a deep love for racing that would carry him through the years.

He became the first Brazilian driver to secure pole position in Formula One since his idol, Ayrton Senna.

In the 1994 French Grand Prix, Rubens Barrichello achieved a significant milestone by becoming the first Brazilian driver since the late Ayrton Senna to secure pole position in a Formula One race. This accomplishment paid homage to his idol and solidified his place among the elite drivers of his nation.

Rubens Barrichello had a memorable battle with Michael Schumacher at the 2002 Austrian Grand Prix.

The 2002 Austrian Grand Prix witnessed an intense battle between Rubens Barrichello and his Ferrari teammate, Michael Schumacher. In a display of sportsmanship and teamwork, Barrichello slowed down on the final straight to allow Schumacher to take the victory, resulting in a controversial yet touching moment of camaraderie.

He holds the record for the highest number of podium finishes without winning the World Championship.

Throughout his career, Rubens Barrichello achieved an impressive 68 podium finishes, making him the driver with the most top-three finishes to never win the coveted World Championship. This statistic showcases his consistent performances and ability to compete at the highest level.

Barrichello served as a mentor to young drivers after retiring from Formula One.

Following his retirement from Formula One, Rubens Barrichello embraced the role of mentor and coach for young racing talents. He shared his vast knowledge and experience to help groom the next generation of drivers, passing on his legacy and ensuring the future of the sport.
