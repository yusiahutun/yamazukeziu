The 15 Best Cotton Underwear for 2024

Cotton underwear is amazing for breathability, but too often, the idea of frumpy, unstylish cotton panties comes to mind first. But that’s actually far from the truth: These days, cotton underwear exists in all shapes and sizes — from teeny-tiny thongs to full-coverage boxers — so you can get the comfort of the fabric without having to forgo your favorite styles.

That’s good news because cotton has a bunch of properties that make it the perfect fabric for underwear. “Because cotton is a natural fiber, it lets the skin breathe, and has absorbent and wicking properties,” says Dora Lau, founder of lingerie solutions company Dora L. International. “It reduces odors and the risk of skin rashes and discomfort. Cotton is also biodegradable and durable.” If that’s enough to convince you, keep reading for a look at the very best cotton underwear out there right now.

What to Keep in Mind

Cotton Percentage

Most cotton underwear these days is made with part elastane or spandex, though we’ve also included some great 100 percent cotton picks. The fabric blend (or lack thereof) is definitely something to keep in mind when you’re choosing your perfect pair. “Pieces that are 100 percent cotton are breathable and come from natural fibers, so are also recyclable,” says Lightbown. “When blended with elastane, you get the benefits of having extra stretch so the fit will be improved, and it also means they will retain their shape better when washed. However, you forgo the ability for them to be recycled.”

Style

Like with other underwear fabrics, cotton panties come in all sorts of styles, so you don’t have to wear a brief if that’s not your preference (though of course there are some great ones of those out there). Check out our picks from Fruit of the Loom or Nubian Skin for some amazing alternatives.

Fit

When purchasing cotton underwear, make sure to consider if you prefer a tight fit or a little give in your panties. 100 percent cotton styles are more likely to give you a little breathing space if that’s what you’re looking for, while cotton blends can provide a tighter fit. You should also think about whether you’re more comfortable in a low, mid or high-rise panty, as well as consider how much coverage you want in the back and at thigh level.

Your Questions, Answered

What are the benefits of cotton underwear?

Cotton underwear is a great choice for a number of reasons. “Cotton is a breathable, natural fabric and has the benefit of absorbing moisture, meaning less chance of irritation and infection,” says Lightbown. “Synthetic fibers tend to trap heat, sweat and discharge so are worth avoiding when it comes to underwear.”

Is underwear less than 100 percent cotton, like 95 percent or less, just as good?

Cotton underwear is still breathable if it’s not 100 percent cotton, promise. “Cotton combined with elastane can provide a better fit than 100 percent cotton, as it will have some stretch properties,” says Lau. “The spandex helps the material return to its shape and fit to the body more accurately. This is especially true when washing the garment. A bit of spandex can also offer light tummy support.”

Does all cotton underwear give you a VPL?

Absolutely not! “It depends on the cut of the piece and the density of the fabric,” says Lightbown, noting that a mix of cotton and modal can help avoid VPL (our pick from Victoria’s Secret fits the bill).

Meanwhile, Lau explains, “The VPL aspect depends on how the garment is constructed. If the waistband and legs are lightly bonded or sewn with a thin flat elastic, there may be no VPL. Elastic, if it’s sewn too tightly, will give a VPL.” She recommends boy shorts or thongs to be on the safe side, but our picks for briefs from Natori and Intimissimi are also totally VPL-free.
