Feminist pieces to wear on International Women's Day and beyond

We earn a commission for products purchased through some links in this article.

Bella Freud

Bella Freud

Credit: Courtesy

For the third year running, Bella Freud has partnered with the Prince's Trust this International Women's Day. For the project, Freud took inspiration from 'A Documentary HerStory of Women Artists in Revolution', which depicts the work of female artists. In particular, Freud resonated with Theresa Schwartz work titled 'Tigers, Wolves, and Pussycats,' which portrays the difficulties Schwartz experienced as a woman in the art industry: “a lone tiger in the art jungle".

Matilde

Matilde

Credit: Courtesy

Matilde's 'Deusa' ring, exclusively designed for International Women's Day last year, translates as 'goddess' in Portuguese. The ring is beautifully symbolic of female power every year and makes a strong statement. 10 per cent of sales of the ring will go towards Plan International.

Sweaty Betty

Sweaty Betty

Credit: Courtesy

Sweaty Betty's International Women’s Day initiative is simply to buy a bra, donate a bra. For every bra purchased from during 3-9 March, The Sweaty Betty Foundation will donate its stamina bra to a young girl in need. Sweaty Betty wants to provide bras to as many young girls as possible, as Portsmouth University research found that breast concerns whilst exercising are the main reason 73 per cent of 11-18-year-old girls drop out of sports and exercise at school.

Rachel Boston

Rachel Boston

Credit: Courtesy

Throughout the month of March, East-London based fine jeweller Rachel Boston will be donating 20 percent of sales of her chunky 'knife edge' bracelet and matching necklace to Women for Refugee Women: a female-led charity that helps to empower and give a voice to refugee and asylum-seeking women in the UK.

Conscious Citizen

Conscious Citizen

Credit: Courtesy

During 7-13 March, female-founded Conscious Citizen is donating 30 per cent of all online sales to TreeSisters, an organisation that prioritises social change and tropical reforestation, while supporting women globally in their roles in climate action.

So far, TreeSisters has planted 22 million trees across the tropical belt and funded radical change in communities, inspiring female leadership to work towards a positive climate impact.

Minka Jewels

Minka Jewels

Credit: Courtesy

Minka Jewels – a British fine jeweller known for her use of exquisite coloured gemstones – is donating 15 per cent of all sales between 1-12 March to Women for Women UK. The charity helps female war survivors rebuild their lives and build a better future with the organisation's training programmes and support network. Since it was established in 1993, Women for Women UK has helped over 500,000 women across eight countries.

Temperley London

Temperley London

Credit: Courtesy

To celebrate International Women’s Day, Temperley London has partnered with Women for Women International, of which designer Alice Temperley is a long-time supporter and ambassador. To mark the occasion, Temperly London has added two new colourways to the classic Clove Tailoring suit (Sandra Bullock, Nathalie Emmanuel and Erin O’Connor are all fans), with golden mustard and rust shades.

Lil Milan

Lil Milan

Credit: Courtesy

Lil Milan's boob charm has 10 per cent of sales donated to Cerchi d'Acqua, an anti-violence centre based in Italy, where the brand originates. Centre Cerchi d'Acqua has helped almost 12,000 violence-related cases over the past 20 years and helps women find strength. The charm, made from 9k gold, has two white stones and can be added to a variety of Lil Milan chains.
