Rob Lowe, 59, makes the rare move of posing with his younger brother Chad, 55

Rob Lowe made a rare move this weekend.

The 9-1-1: Lone Star actor posed with his younger brother Chad Lowe as both wore dark suits.

Rob, 59, and Chad, 55, were seen smiling at the camera while side-by-side as the post was captioned, 'We clean up nice.'

Both Rob and Chad are actors.

Rob came to fame as a Brat Packer in the 1980s with St Elmo's Fire and About Last night while Chad earned raves for his TV series Life Goes On which aired from 1989 until 1993.

In March Chad posed with his 13-year-old daughter Mabel Painter Lowe whose mother is Chad's wife Kim Painter.

Chad was in 1991's Highway To Hell, 2006's Beautiful Ohio and the Hailey Dean Mystery from 2016 until 2019.

And he even worked on Melrose Place in the role of Carter Gallavan, a computer software billionaire. 

Chad was wed to Hilary Swank from 1997 until 2007; she won her Oscars for the films Boys Don't Cry in 1999 and for Million Dollar Baby in 2004.

And she continues to be an A list star - the actress, who is pregnant with twins, is now on the show Alaska Daily. 

Mom Kim has also worked in Hollywood, but as a crew member. Her credits included Enemy Of The State, Ted Bundy, and Instant Dads.

Rob was seen with his wife of over 30 years, Sheryl Berkoff, 61, as they posed alongside their two handsome sons at the premiere of Unstable.

The star showed off his chiseled good looks in a navy blue suit over a navy shirt with a herringbone pattern and styled his short brown hair spiked and messy.

The veteran actor's latest project is a family affair, as he teamed up with his youngest son John Owen Lowe, 28, for a new Netflix comedy series they co-created together.

Both Rob and John Owen star in the series and serve as executive producers on the project.

It's a family affair: Rob and his wife, Sheryl Berkoff posed with their sons Matthew Edward Lowe (left), and John Owen Lowe, at the premiere of Unstable

The father-son duo co-created the 10-episode comedy series alongside Santa Monica Diet creator Victor Fresco, who also serves as executive producer.

'I never thought I would see my son and I co-creating a TV series and anybody out there who gets to work with their kids knows what a gift that is. It's a whole other layer of your relationship,' The Pentaverate actor told Parade in October.

Rob plays Ellis, the 'wildly eccentric' CFO of a biotech research company, and Johnny plays his son Jackson who goes to work for him.

The plot will follow John's character, the 'socially-challenged son' who goes to work with his father 'to save himself from disaster.'

SNL alum Fred Armisen has a supporting role in the Jay Chandrasekhar-helmed series as Leslie, Ellis' board-appointed therapist who is desperate to be liked.

'We are very excited to be working with Victor, Rob, and John Owen,” said Netflix Head of Comedy Tracey Pakosta.   

'The three of them – and their wildly funny, smart sensibilities – are a perfect fit for our growing slate of scripted comedies,' Pakosta added.

John Owen had previously starred alongside his father on Fox's The Grinder and the 2019 Netflix film Holiday in the Wild.
