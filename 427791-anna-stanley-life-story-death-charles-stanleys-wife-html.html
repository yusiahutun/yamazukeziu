Divorce

Charles and Anna were married for four decades before getting divorced. The two had been in a troubling marriage for years before the marriage annulment was granted.

In his book, Deep and Wide, the couple's son Andy gave a peek into his parents’ marriage. He said that the union had been dead long before the divorce. He also described the couple's long efforts at marital counselling with trained therapists.

Trouble started when the couple moved to Atlanta, Georgia. Charles allegedly became too busy with ministry work and neglected his family as a result.

His wife felt hurt and rejected. As a result, she moved out of their house and stopped going to her husband's church.

Despite the prevailing challenges, the couple was opposed to divorce. Neither wanted to initiate it. The two had been married for about 38 years when she first filed for divorce in 1993.

She later amended the file to a request for separate maintenance, which is a form of legal separation.

The couple could not agree on the terms of the legal separation, so she refiled for legal separation again in March 1995 and asked for a jury trial. Efforts to reconcile the two were futile.

The divorce process was long and tedious, and it affected the running of the church Charles pastored. As a result, he committed to resigning once the divorce proceedings were finalised, but church members chose to retain him as long as he did not remarry.

The divorce process was concluded and announced on 16th February 2000. Charles continued with his ministry until 2020, when he felt it was time to step aside. He passed away in April 2023 at 90.
