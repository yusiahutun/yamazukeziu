9 Interesting Facts About Venezuela and Its Incredible People

  • While many people see Venezuela as a struggling economy in Latin America, others also know it for having beautiful beaches
  • Venezuela was once South America's richest country, but that changed after corruption, and other problems came in
  • People in the country speak more than 35 languages, with Chinese and Italian being the most common foreign languages

To many people, Venezuela is a country nestled in Latin America with a very rich story. People from all over the world visit the country to experience the wonders of the Caribbean islands.

For another group, Venezuela is all about a struggling economy. Facts.Net reports that the situation has caused many people to leave the country and seek greener pastures elsewhere.

Below are more facts about Venezuela:

1. Venezuelans speak more than 35 different languages

Spanish became Venezuela's official language after the declaration of the 1999 Constitution of Venezuela. Apart from Spanish, Venezuelans use more than 35 different languages. The most widely-spoken indigenous languages in the country are Manduhuaca, Yanomami, Warao, and Guahibo, among others.

2. The flag was designed by a military leader

Originally, Venezuela's flag bore a horizontal tricolour of yellow, blue, and red. The new version of the flag now includes an arc of eight stars and the National Coat of Arms in the upper left corner, which was introduced in 2006.

The flag was designed by military leader and revolutionary Francisco de Miranda in 1806. Miranda was also credited with the independence of Latin America as a whole. The National Congress approved the flag that he made in 1811.

3. Chinese language is common in Venezuela

After Spanish, Chinese, Italian, and Portuguese are the most spoken languages in Venezuela. Wayuu is the most spoken indigenous language with more than 150,000 speakers.

4. Venezuela was South America's richest country

Unknown to many, Venezuela used to be the richest country in South America. However, the last decade has seen corruption and failed government policies push the country’s economy to collapse. This was followed by crippled infrastructure with millions of Venezuelans in poverty. As of 2020, Venezuela's GDP had reached $250 billion.

5. Pastelitos are breakfast staple

Among the treats in the country, Venezuelans love to eat pastelitos for breakfast. Pastelitos is a pastry made with wheat flour and filled with chicken or cheese.

6. Heavy money printing caused Venezuela's hyperinflation

According to experts, Venezuela started experiencing hyperinflation during the first year of Nicolás Maduro’s presidency in 2014. Deficit spending and heavy money printing are among the major causes of hyperinflation.

7. Tissue paper costs about KSh 395

As of 2018, experts estimated that tissue paper in the country would cost KSh 395 because of inflation, which peaked at 1 million per cent. The toilet paper roll would have cost around 40 cents in US dollars.

8. More than 60 beaches in Isla de Margarita

Isla de Margarita is filled with first-class beaches with excellent island activities. With a 171km coastline, around 60 beaches can be found on Isla de Margarita.

Popular beaches include Playa Punta Arenas, Playa Parguito, Playa Puerto Cruz, Playa Caribe, Playa El Yaque, and Playa El Agua. Furthermore, its towns also carry historic architecture, museums, colourful houses, cafes, and shops.

9. Venezuela is one of the world's biggest oil producers

Venezuela's economy largely depends on petroleum and other manufacturing sectors. Despite having a struggling economy since 2010, Venezuela remains one of the biggest members of the Organisation of the Petroleum Exporting Countries (OPEC).
